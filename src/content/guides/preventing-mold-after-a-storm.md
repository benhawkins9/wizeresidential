Gulf Coast storms don't knock politely. When a tropical system or severe thunderstorm pushes water into your Ocean Springs home — through the roof, around windows, under doors, or up from the ground — the clock starts immediately. Mold in our climate doesn't wait for you to figure out next steps.

Here's what I tell homeowners across Jackson County after a storm event: move fast, move smart, and don't let the storm damage become a mold problem that outlasts the repair.

## The First 24 Hours Are Critical

The moment it's safe to be inside, your goal is to stop moisture from sitting in building materials. That means:

- **Remove standing water** with wet vacs, pumps, or whatever you have available.
- **Pull wet materials fast.** Saturated carpet, padding, and lower sections of drywall hold moisture for days. If they're soaked through, they need to come out — not dry in place.
- **Open windows and run fans** to create cross-ventilation. Bring in dehumidifiers if you have them or can rent them.
- **Don't seal the house up and just run the AC.** Central air isn't designed to dry out wet structural materials. You need mechanical drying with real airflow.

## Finding Hidden Moisture

This is where a lot of mold problems start. Water that enters during a storm doesn't stay where you can see it. It migrates inside walls, under flooring, into insulation, and into ceiling cavities. You may think you dried out the room when the moisture is still active somewhere you can't see.

Signs of hidden moisture after a storm:

- Musty smell that doesn't improve with ventilation
- Soft or discolored drywall above or below the intrusion point
- Buckled or warped flooring
- Paint bubbling days after the storm

A moisture meter is the only reliable way to confirm dryness in walls and floors. If you're doing your own assessment, probe materials — don't just look at surfaces.

## Why Bleach Isn't the Answer

I hear this regularly: "I sprayed bleach on it, so it should be fine." On ceramic tile or glass, bleach works. On drywall, wood framing, or concrete block — which is what most Ocean Springs homes are built with — bleach doesn't penetrate to where mold is actually growing. It kills what's on the surface and adds moisture to the problem.

For mold prevention after a storm, EPA-registered antimicrobials and botanical-based treatments are formulated to address porous materials where surface sprays fall short. That's what I use in [mold remediation work](/services/mold-remediation) — not a bottle from the hardware store.

## When to Call for Help

DIY mold cleanup is reasonable for small, isolated surface areas on non-porous materials. But call a licensed, insured professional (MS #1500778) if:

- The affected area is larger than about 10 square feet
- Mold is growing inside wall cavities or under flooring
- You can't identify where the moisture is coming from
- Anyone in the house has asthma, allergies, or a compromised immune system

Mold that isn't fully remediated doesn't disappear — it grows back. Half-measures after a storm often mean full remediation later, at greater cost.
