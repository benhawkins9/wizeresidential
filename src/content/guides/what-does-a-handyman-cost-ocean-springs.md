One of the most common questions I get: "What's this going to cost me?" It's a fair question, and I'd rather answer it plainly upfront than have you guessing.

Here's how pricing works at Wise Residential.

## The $90 Service Visit

For small jobs, there's a $90 service visit fee. That covers:

- Travel to your location
- Loading and unloading tools
- Setup on site
- The first 1 hour and 15 minutes of work

For a lot of small repairs — a running toilet, a stuck door, a loose fixture — that's all it takes. You pay $90 plus materials, and the job's done.

This is how small-job handyman work stays viable. I can't absorb an hour of drive time and setup for a 20-minute repair without some kind of base fee. The alternative is refusing small jobs or inflating the hourly rate — neither of which is fair to you.

## Flat-Rate Estimates for Bigger Jobs

For anything that's going to run longer than a service visit — a drywall repair, a bathroom fixture swap, installing a new door, cleaning gutters on a two-story house — I'll give you a written flat-rate estimate before I touch anything.

You know the number, I know the number. No surprises on the invoice.

The estimate accounts for my time and the scope of the work. Materials are always listed and priced separately at actual cost. I don't mark up materials to pad the bill.

## Half-Day and Full-Day Blocks

If you've got a list of things to tackle — and a lot of homeowners do — a half-day or full-day block is often the most efficient way to get through it. We go down the list, knock out what we can, and you get multiple items handled in a single visit.

This is also how I handle jobs that are scoped but hard to time precisely. Rather than booking by the task and running over, we agree on a block of time and work through the scope together.

## What Affects the Cost

A few things that move the number:

- **Height and access** — anything requiring a ladder, working in a tight crawl space, or accessing a second-story roofline takes more time and care
- **Condition of existing materials** — a door that's out of square because the frame has shifted takes longer than a straightforward rehang
- **Materials** — I'll tell you what's needed and give you the choice to source it yourself or have me pick it up

## The Smartest Way to Save Money: Batch Your Jobs

If you've been putting off five small repairs, scheduling them as five separate visits means paying the service visit fee five times. Schedule them together — or book a half-day block — and that overhead gets absorbed into a single trip.

I recommend making a list before you call. Walk through the house, note everything that's been bothering you, and we'll figure out what can be done in one visit. You'll almost always come out ahead.
