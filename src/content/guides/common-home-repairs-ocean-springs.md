Living on the Gulf Coast means your home works harder than most. Salt air, high humidity, storm season, and temperature swings put real wear on a house year after year. After working on homes throughout Ocean Springs, Gautier, St. Martin, and the surrounding Jackson County area, I've seen the same problems come up again and again. Here's what Gulf Coast homes need most.

## 1. Wood Rot Repair

This is the number-one issue I see on the coast. Moisture gets into window frames, door jambs, soffits, fascia, and porch columns — and once rot starts, it spreads fast. Catching it early is almost always cheaper than waiting. Learn more about what drives this on [our carpentry and wood rot service page](/services/carpentry-wood-rot).

## 2. Drywall Patching and Repair

Holes from door handles, moisture stains, settling cracks, and storm damage are all common. Drywall repair done right means the patch disappears — you shouldn't be able to tell it was ever there. See [drywall and painting services](/services/drywall-painting) for what's included.

## 3. Doors and Windows That Stick or Won't Seal

Humidity causes wood to swell and frames to shift. Doors that drag, windows that won't latch, and gaps in weatherstripping all create energy loss and security issues. These are straightforward fixes that make a noticeable difference. Details at [door and window services](/services/door-window).

## 4. Plumbing Fixture Repairs

Dripping faucets, running toilets, loose supply lines, and worn shut-off valves fall into the category of small jobs that add up on your water bill if ignored. Most fixture repairs take under an hour.

## 5. Mold and Mildew

Ocean Springs has the humidity to grow mold fast — especially after a wet spell or any water intrusion. I handle mold remediation on a small to mid-size scale. If you've noticed musty odors or visible spots, [mold remediation services](/services/mold-remediation) covers the process.

## 6. Caulking and Weatherstripping

Failed caulk around tubs, windows, and exterior penetrations lets water and insects in. This is one of the cheapest repairs on the list and one of the highest-value ones in terms of preventing bigger problems later.

## 7. Fascia, Soffit, and Exterior Trim

Gulf Coast storms beat up the edges of a roofline. Damaged fascia and soffit allow moisture and pests into the attic structure. These repairs overlap with wood rot work and are best addressed before the next storm season.

## 8. Interior Paint and Wall Finish

Humidity causes paint to peel, especially in bathrooms and kitchens. A fresh coat after a proper surface prep makes a home feel maintained and protects the drywall underneath.

## 9. Deck and Porch Boards

Elevated moisture and UV exposure wear out decking faster on the Gulf Coast than in drier climates. Replacing rotted boards and re-securing loose ones is a safety issue, not just cosmetic.

## 10. Small Electrical and Fixture Issues

Ceiling fan wobble, light fixture replacements, and outlet cover repairs are the kinds of tasks that sit on a homeowner's list for months. Handling several of these in one visit is what [small job services](/services/small-jobs) are designed for.
