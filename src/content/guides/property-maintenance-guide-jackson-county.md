Owning a home in Jackson County — whether you're in Ocean Springs, Gautier, St. Martin, Vancleave, or Moss Point — means dealing with conditions that wear on a house differently than they would inland. Humidity stays high most of the year. Salt air works on anything metal or wood that's exposed to the outside. Storm season runs from June through November and doesn't always announce itself. A maintenance routine built around those realities protects your investment and keeps repair costs manageable.

## Spring: Storm Season Preparation (March–May)

Before the first named storm of the season, walk the exterior and check:

- **Wood trim, soffits, and fascia** for soft spots or paint failure — these are entry points for water and insects
- **Caulk around windows, doors, and exterior penetrations** — Gulf Coast UV and heat break down caulk faster than most manufacturers' estimates suggest
- **Gutters and downspouts** — clear debris and confirm downspouts direct water away from the foundation
- **Crawl space vents** — confirm screens are intact to keep pests out during wet months
- **Door and window seals** — anything that doesn't latch, seal, or open smoothly should be addressed before hurricane season

[Carpentry and wood rot repairs](/services/carpentry-wood-rot) are most efficiently done in spring, before the heat and rain of summer set in.

## Summer: Humidity and Moisture Watch (June–August)

High humidity is the norm, and storm activity picks up. During summer months, focus on:

- **Interior moisture** — check under sinks, around tub and shower caulk lines, and near the water heater for any slow leaks or mineral buildup
- **Water quality** — hard water and Gulf Coast mineral content can affect your fixtures, appliances, and the water you drink. [Whole-home water filtration](/services/water-filtration) reduces fixture wear and improves water quality year-round
- **Mold and mildew** — musty smells or visible spots, especially in bathrooms, closets, or crawl spaces, should be addressed quickly. Mold spreads fast in this climate

## Fall: Post-Storm Assessment (September–November)

After storm season activity winds down, do a thorough walk:

- Inspect the roof edge, fascia, and any wood trim that took wind or debris exposure
- Check window and door frames for movement or new gaps — seasonal swelling can leave gaps that allow air and moisture infiltration
- Look at decking and porch boards for fasteners that have worked loose or boards that have cupped or cracked
- Clean gutters again after fall leaf drop

## Winter: Fixtures and Interior Systems (December–February)

Jackson County winters are mild, but this is the best time for interior work:

- Address plumbing fixtures that have been dripping or running — small leaks waste water and add up on your bill
- Repaint or touch up interior walls where humidity caused peeling through the summer
- Schedule any [drywall repairs](/services/drywall-painting) or door and window adjustments before spring humidity returns

## Build a Relationship With One Contractor

The most efficient approach to property maintenance is working with a single licensed contractor who knows your home. When I've been to a house before, I already know what was repaired last season, what's coming up, and what to watch for. That continuity saves time and often catches problems earlier.
