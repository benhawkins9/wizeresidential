When something breaks or needs attention around the house, the first question most homeowners ask isn't "how do I fix this?" It's "who do I call?" In Mississippi, the two most common answers are a handyman or a general contractor — and choosing the wrong one can cost you time and money.

Here's how I think about it, from the field.

## What a Handyman Does

A handyman handles individual repairs and improvements that don't require coordinating multiple licensed trades. Think of it as skilled, self-contained work: patching drywall, adjusting sticky doors, replacing trim, caulking windows, repairing fences, swapping out light fixtures, and dozens of other jobs that pile up in any home.

Most of what homeowners in Ocean Springs, Gautier, and Biloxi actually need falls squarely in this category — work that's too specific to DIY confidently but too small to justify a full contractor.

When you search for a [handyman near me](/services/small-jobs), you want someone who shows up, does the work cleanly, and doesn't tack on overhead for project management you don't need.

## What a General Contractor Does

A GC is the right hire when a project involves multiple licensed subcontractors — electricians, plumbers, HVAC techs — working in sequence. Full kitchen remodels, room additions, major structural repairs: these need someone to pull permits, schedule subs, and manage the overall build.

GCs earn their fee on complex projects. But that fee — typically a percentage markup over all labor and materials — doesn't make sense when you need three things patched and a door rehung.

## The Overlap: Licensed Handyman Work

Here's where it gets practical. A licensed handyman in Mississippi can legally perform a wide range of repairs that sit just below the threshold requiring a GC. That includes most carpentry, drywall, painting, door and window work, and general maintenance.

As a licensed home repair contractor in MS (#1500778), I handle exactly that middle ground — jobs too involved for a weekend DIY attempt, but not complex enough to warrant contractor overhead.

## How to Decide

Ask yourself two questions:

- **Does the job require more than one licensed trade?** If yes, you may need a GC.
- **Can one skilled person complete it in a day or two?** If yes, a handyman is the better fit — and the better value.

For most Ocean Springs homeowners, the answer is a qualified handyman with a solid list of [small jobs](/services/small-jobs) they handle well.
