Hiring someone to work on your home is a real decision. You're letting a stranger onto your property, often while you're there, and trusting them with something you've built equity in. Here's how I'd evaluate a contractor — including how I'd evaluate myself.

## Check License and Insurance First

In Mississippi, licensing requirements exist for a reason. Before anything else, ask for:

- The contractor's Mississippi license number
- Proof of general liability insurance

Both protect you. A license tells you the contractor has met the state's standards. Insurance means that if something is damaged during the job — your property or a worker — you're not on the hook. Wise Residential is licensed (MS #1500778) and insured. Any legitimate local handyman services provider should be able to hand you this information without hesitation.

## Get a Written Estimate Before Work Starts

Verbal estimates lead to disputes. A written estimate should spell out:

- What work will be done (scope)
- What materials will be used
- What the total cost is, or a clear not-to-exceed number
- An expected timeline

If a contractor resists putting it in writing, that's a signal. At [/estimate](/estimate), I offer free written estimates with no pressure.

## Make Sure They Handle Your Job Size

Some contractors won't touch small jobs. Others only do large renovations. A [small job service](/services/small-jobs) built for repairs — not construction — means the contractor isn't trying to upsell you into a project bigger than what you need. Ask directly: "Do you take repair jobs under $500?" You'll find out quickly whether you've found the right fit.

## Pay Attention to Communication

How a contractor communicates before you hire them is a preview of how they'll communicate during and after the job. If calls go unreturned for two days, that's what you'll get when a question comes up mid-project.

Things to notice:
- Do they answer or return calls promptly?
- Do they explain what they plan to do in plain language?
- Do they ask enough questions to understand your problem before quoting?

## Watch for These Red Flags

- No license or insurance, or resistance to showing proof
- Asking for a large cash deposit before any work begins
- No written estimate or contract
- Showing up after a storm offering deep discounts for immediate work
- Vague answers about timeline or materials

A contractor familiar with Ocean Springs, Gautier, D'Iberville, and Pascagoula homes understands what the humidity, salt air, and storm season actually do to structures here. That's different from a general handyman who's never worked coastal Mississippi. Local experience means fewer surprises.
