Summer on the Gulf Coast doesn't sneak up on you. By June the heat and humidity are already at full swing, and by July your home's water system is working harder than it did all winter. More showers, lawn irrigation running, ice makers cycling — your water filters take the brunt of it.

Spring is the right time to get ahead of that. A quick check of your filtration system before peak season beats a mid-August call because the water pressure dropped or the filter housing cracked from pressure buildup.

## Why Summer Is Harder on Filters

Household water consumption rises significantly in warmer months. When more water moves through a filter in a shorter period of time, cartridges reach their capacity faster than the calendar suggests they should.

If you changed your filter in October and planned to swap it again in spring, that schedule makes sense. But if you had a wet spring with high irrigation use, or you're hosting more people than usual during summer months, your filter may be closer to done than the date on the housing tells you.

## What to Check Before the Heat Peaks

Before temperatures are consistently in the 90s — which in Ocean Springs and across the MS Gulf Coast usually means no later than early June — run through these:

- **Sediment pre-filter:** This is usually the first cartridge in a whole-house system. Hold it up to light. If it's noticeably brown or gray, swap it.
- **Carbon block filter:** Look for the change date you wrote on the housing. If it's been 6+ months and summer usage is coming, replace it now rather than in the middle of a heat wave.
- **Filter housing:** Check the housing body for hairline cracks, especially at the lid threads. The pressure cycles of heavy summer use can worsen small cracks quickly.
- **Flow rate:** Run a cold tap and notice the pressure. A meaningful drop in flow is often the first sign a filter is due.

## A Note for Well-Water Households in Vancleave

If your home in Vancleave or rural Jackson County runs on a private well, your filtration situation is different from a municipal water connection. Well water in this area can carry sediment, iron, and other minerals that vary by location and season. Wet-season runoff can temporarily change what's coming through your well casing.

For [water filtration](/services/water-filtration) on a private well, I'd recommend checking your sediment pre-filter monthly during high-usage months rather than waiting for a set interval. Discolored water or reduced flow are the clearest signals something needs attention.

## Vacation Homes Need Extra Attention

Vacation and second homes along the Gulf Coast often sit unused for weeks or months, then see heavy use in summer. Filters that sat stagnant can harbor bacterial growth. Before occupying a home that's been closed up, flush each tap for several minutes and check the filter condition before relying on it.
