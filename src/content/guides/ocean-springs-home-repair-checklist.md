Coastal living is good for the soul and hard on a house. Salt air, high humidity, and Gulf storms put Mississippi homes through more wear than most people account for — until something fails at the wrong time.

The fix isn't constant renovation. It's staying ahead with small, regular repairs. This checklist is designed for Ocean Springs homeowners who want to keep their home solid without letting small problems turn into big bills.

## Exterior Checklist

Walk the outside of your home twice a year — spring and fall — and look for:

- **Caulking** around all windows, doors, and trim. Gulf Coast sun and humidity degrade caulk fast. If it's cracking, peeling, or pulling away, water is finding its way in.
- **Wood trim and fascia boards.** Probe with a screwdriver. Soft spots mean rot has started. Catching it early is inexpensive; waiting is not.
- **Gutters and downspouts.** Clear of debris and directing water away from the foundation? If not, you're inviting moisture problems.
- **Screens.** Torn screens let in the Gulf Coast's mosquitoes and pests. Easy fix, often overlooked.
- **Fence posts and gates.** Check for lean, soft wood at the base, and hardware that's seized or rusted.
- **Exterior paint and siding.** Bubbling or peeling paint usually means moisture is underneath it, not just old paint.

## Interior Checklist

Inside, focus on anything moisture, movement, or time has stressed:

- **Doors that stick or don't latch.** Usually a simple adjustment. Left alone, they get worse and can become a security issue.
- **Drywall cracks and nail pops.** Common in Gulf Coast homes that shift with humidity. Small patches now, not big repairs later.
- **Caulk around tubs, showers, and sinks.** Failed caulk here leads directly to water damage and mold behind the wall.
- **Cabinet hinges and drawer slides.** Worn hardware is a quick fix that makes a daily difference.
- **Attic ventilation and insulation.** Check for moisture staining, which signals a leak or condensation problem.

## Pre-Storm Prep Additions

Before hurricane season hits — and it always hits — add these to your list:

- Loose trim, shutters, or siding that could become projectiles
- Door sweeps and weatherstripping that seal out wind-driven rain
- Any tree limbs close enough to contact the house
