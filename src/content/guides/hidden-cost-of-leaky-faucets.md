A dripping faucet is easy to tune out. It starts as background noise and stays that way for weeks or months while it quietly runs up your water bill and does damage you can't see yet.

I've been in a lot of homes in Ocean Springs, Gautier, and Moss Point where a "minor" drip turned into a cabinet full of mold or a subfloor that had to be replaced. The repair wasn't minor anymore at that point.

## What the Water Waste Actually Adds Up To

A single faucet dripping once per second wastes a meaningful amount of water every year — not a trivial amount. Multiply that across two or three leaky fixtures and you're looking at a measurable line item on your monthly water bill every month you leave them alone.

A running toilet is worse. When the flapper doesn't seat properly, water runs continuously from the tank to the bowl in a loop. It's quiet enough to ignore, and the bill is the only place you notice it — until the floor around the base starts to soften.

## The Hidden Damage Risk

The water bill is the obvious cost. The hidden cost is what the water does to your home while it's dripping.

Under-sink leaks soak cabinet bases and subflooring. Slow seeps around faucet handles work their way behind tile or drywall. In Gulf Coast homes where humidity stays high most of the year, that moisture doesn't dry out between drips — it accumulates, and mold follows.

By the time you smell it or see it, the damage has been building for weeks. [Mold remediation](/services/mold-remediation) on a cabinet base or wall cavity is far more involved and more expensive than replacing a faucet cartridge would have been. The math isn't close.

## Why "I'll Get to It Eventually" Doesn't Work Here

I understand the impulse to put off small repairs. Life is busy. The faucet still works, technically.

But a drip doesn't plateau — it either gets fixed or it gets worse. Worn washers continue to wear. A slow drip under pressure can become a steady stream. And every week it goes unfixed is another week of water damage accumulating in a place you can't see.
